What is a Snowstorm?

A snowstorm is a weather condition where there is heavy snowfall accompanied by strong winds, reducing visibility and creating hazardous conditions. Snowstorms can lead to extreme cold temperatures and cause disruptions to travel, power outages, and other problems. Snowstorms can occur in winter months in cold climates, but they can happen in other seasons as well.

Types of Snowstorms:

  • Blizzard: A severe snowstorm with strong winds, low visibility, and very cold temperatures.
  • Snow Squall: A brief but intense snowstorm that can cause whiteout conditions.
  • Lake-effect Snowstorm: Occurs when cold air moves over a warm body of water, creating heavy snowfall.

Impact of Snowstorms:

  • Disruption to travel and transportation.
  • Increased risk of hypothermia and frostbite.
  • Power outages due to downed trees and power lines.
  • Road closures and accidents due to poor visibility and slippery conditions.
  • Economic disruptions due to storm-related shutdowns.

Before a Snowstorm

  • Check weather forecasts and prepare for the storm.
  • Stock up on emergency supplies, including blankets, food, and water.
  • Ensure heating systems and generators are working properly.
  • Clear gutters and drainpipes to prevent ice damming.

During a Snowstorm

  • Stay indoors as much as possible.
  • If traveling, drive slowly and use your headlights.
  • Keep warm and avoid prolonged exposure to cold temperatures.
  • Check on elderly neighbors and loved ones.

After a Snowstorm

  • Shovel snow and ice from walkways and driveways.
  • Check for damage to structures and report it to authorities.
  • Clear fallen branches and trees from roads and driveways.
