What is a Landslide?

A landslide is the movement of rock, soil, and debris down a slope due to gravity. Landslides can be triggered by factors like heavy rainfall, earthquakes, volcanic activity, or human activity such as deforestation. They often occur in hilly or mountainous regions and can be dangerous, causing property damage and loss of life.

Types of Landslides:

  • Rockfall: Sudden fall of rock or debris from a steep slope.
  • Mudslides: Rapid downslope movement of water-saturated soil and debris.
  • Slumps: Slow downward movement of a section of the slope.

Impact of Landslides:

  • Destruction of homes and infrastructure.
  • Blockage of roads and transportation routes.
  • Loss of life and injury due to falling debris.
  • Soil erosion and long-term environmental damage.

Before a Landslide

  • Identify landslide-prone areas near your home or workplace.
  • Secure objects on slopes to prevent them from falling.
  • Install retaining walls or barriers to prevent soil erosion.
  • Monitor local weather conditions and watch for heavy rainfall.

During a Landslide

  • If outdoors, move to higher ground immediately.
  • If indoors, stay away from windows and doors.
  • Do not attempt to cross areas affected by landslides.
  • Listen to emergency alerts and evacuate if necessary.

After a Landslide

  • Do not enter areas where landslides have occurred until authorities declare them safe.
  • Inspect your home for damage and repair any structural issues.
  • Help clear debris and support community recovery efforts.
