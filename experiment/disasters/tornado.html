What is a Tornado?

A tornado is a rapidly rotating column of air that is in contact with both the Earth's surface and a cumulonimbus cloud or, in rare cases, the base of a cumulus cloud. Tornadoes are among the most violent storms and can cause massive damage to buildings and infrastructure. They are often accompanied by thunderstorms and can form in regions with specific weather conditions.

Types of Tornadoes:

  • Supercell Tornadoes: The most powerful and destructive type, formed by severe thunderstorms.
  • Non-Supercell Tornadoes: Smaller tornadoes that form outside of supercell thunderstorms.

Impact of Tornadoes:

  • Severe damage to buildings, vehicles, and infrastructure.
  • Flying debris that can cause injury or death.
  • Loss of power and disruption to services.
  • Loss of life and injury from high winds and falling objects.

Before a Tornado

  • Monitor weather updates and be aware of tornado watches or warnings.
  • Have an emergency kit with essentials like water, food, and medical supplies.
  • Know the safest place in your home (basement, storm cellar, or interior room).
  • Secure outdoor objects that could become projectiles.

During a Tornado

  • Take shelter in a basement or interior room on the lowest level.
  • Do not stay in a mobile home or vehicle.
  • Cover your head and neck to protect from flying debris.
  • Stay away from windows and doors.

After a Tornado

  • Wait for official authorities to declare it safe to leave your shelter.
  • Be cautious of downed power lines and gas leaks.
  • Help with search and rescue efforts if safe to do so.
  • Stay informed about recovery efforts and available support.
