What is a Wildfire?

A wildfire is an uncontrolled fire that burns in natural vegetation like forests, grasslands, and shrublands. These fires can spread rapidly and are often caused by human activities, lightning, or extreme heat. Wildfires can be highly destructive, threatening wildlife, property, and human lives.

Types of Wildfires:

  • Ground Fires: Fires that burn in the lower layers of the soil, including grass and roots.
  • Surface Fires: Fires that burn in vegetation and shrubs near the surface of the ground.
  • Crown Fires: Fires that spread in the canopy of trees and are the most dangerous.

Impact of Wildfires:

  • Destruction of homes, infrastructure, and natural habitats.
  • Loss of life and injuries from smoke inhalation or flames.
  • Displacement of communities due to evacuation orders.
  • Environmental damage, including the loss of trees and wildlife.
  • Air quality deterioration and the release of harmful gases.

Before a Wildfire

  • Stay informed by monitoring fire weather reports and alerts.
  • Create a defensible space around your home by clearing dry vegetation.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essential items like water, food, masks, and medications.
  • Know multiple evacuation routes and keep your vehicle fueled.

During a Wildfire

  • If you are outdoors, try to move to a safe location away from the fire.
  • If you are indoors, close windows and doors, and stay indoors to avoid smoke inhalation.
  • Follow evacuation instructions and leave early to avoid being trapped.
  • Wear protective clothing, such as a long-sleeved shirt, pants, and a mask if possible.

After a Wildfire

  • Wait for official authorities to declare it safe to return home.
  • Check for damage and report hazards like fallen power lines or structural damage.
  • Assist in firefighting and cleanup efforts if safe to do so.
  • Take care of respiratory health by avoiding exposure to smoke and ash.
